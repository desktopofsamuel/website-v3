Reflecting on 2025, there's steady progress in the places that mattered: **career transition, community, and staying physically active**. Here's what I did, what I'm proud of, and what I'm taking into next year.

![Cover image: 2025 Wrapped](/static/20260125-2025-wrapped-1.png)

## Things I Did & Proud This Year

### Recovering to Full Speed

After leaving OKX at the end of last year, I took a month-long break. I attended **World Cafe on Career Break**, which gave me a lot of insight into what a break could really mean. During that period, I traveled, read, went on random adventures with my camera, and did quite a lot of journaling. 

### Journaling

I kept up journaling this year—my Muji notebook is nearly full now. Even though I believe my blog has a small audience, I met a reader at a random event this year. Those moments remind me why I keep writing. 

### Golf, Pickleball, Running, Tennis

One of my resolutions was to be **more physically active** in 2025. I'd say that goal was fulfilled. I joined a government-subsidized golf class at a very low cost in March, joined a running class, and started personal training in November with my wife. I also kept playing tennis, which has become something I want to get good at. 

### Community

Another year with **Friends of Figma Hong Kong**—and a very productive one. We launched our **inaugural mentorship program**, and I hosted **World Tour**, a virtual event connecting FoF chapters around the world, which also had an in-person component. Highlights:

- Inaugural mentorship program
- World Tour ([watch on YouTube](https://youtu.be/oOXpVqCVg6k))
- Design system work

## Personal Website Revamp

After years of procrastination, I finally upgraded my site from the Next.js Pages Router to the **App Router** (introduced back in 2023). Most of the design looks the same, but underneath it was a major refactor and rewrite of the entire codebase. Thanks to **Cursor**, the migration was relatively painless—it understood Next.js docs and best practices well. I also moved off Chakra to **Tailwind CSS** (with native OKLCH support) for more flexibility.

My writing was also recognized this year. My site was featured as [Portfolio of the Day](https://app.designerdailyreport.com) on Designer Daily Report, and my post **"OKLCH, Explained for Designers"** was featured in [Sidebar](https://sidebar.io)—imagine the surprise of reading my own blog in a newsletter I subscribe to. 

## Best of 2025

### Samsung Galaxy Fold 7

This year I switched back to Android (again—my last Android was the Pixel 2) after the release of the iPhone 17 Pro. I really struggled with the direction of iPhone this year and was drawn to multitasking, Google Gemini on a phone, and the foldable form factor. I made the call. A few months later, I haven't regretted it. 

### LocalSend

**LocalSend** has been a seamless, near-native experience for cross-platform users like me—sending files between iOS, macOS, Android, and Windows with no hassle. 

### Chess.com

I started learning chess on Duolingo (one of the AI projects the company kicked off), but after a 160-day streak I found the puzzles sometimes flawed and not following the best moves. I switched to **Chess.com**.

I really enjoy the coach and review features; that's exactly how I want to learn a new skill. Duolingo's gamification is strong, but a lot of the exercises felt repetitive.

### Obsidian

After years away, I'm back on **Obsidian**—revisiting it for note-taking and thinking.

## Next Year

### How I Use Tools

2025 felt like a tools explosion—the year of AI—and **cognitive overload** is real. The cost of building software has dropped a lot, but so has my patience for juggling too many apps.

One of my learnings: **pick a single tool with strong interoperability**. With AI, context matters more than ever. The old PARA idea of one structure across every tool has lost some of its edge. I'm leaning into fewer, better-connected tools.
